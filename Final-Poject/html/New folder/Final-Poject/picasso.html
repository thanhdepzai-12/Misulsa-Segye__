PABLO PICASSO

ChildHood

From an early age, Picasso showed a passion and aptitude in the field of painting, according to his mother, the first word that Pablo said was "piz", short for "lápiz" ", which in Spanish means pencil. Picasso's father was a realist bird painter, and José was also an art instructor and curator of the local museum, Barcelona's School of Visual Arts. And Picasso received formal painting training from his father when he was 7 years old.

Life Again

Picasso dropped out of school to go to Paris, the art center of Europe at that time. In the French capital, he lived with Max Jacob, a journalist and poet who helped Pablo learn French. This was a difficult time for the young artist when he had to live in poverty, cold and sometimes despair, most of Pablo's work had to be burned to warm their small room.

After the liberation of Paris in 1944, then aged 63, Picasso began an affair with a young art student, Françoise Gilot. Françoise and Picasso had two children together, Claude and Paloma. Different from the artist's other lovers, it was Françoise who left Pablo in 1953. This was a shock to Picasso, he thought he was getting old and had become odd in the eyes of women, Some of his works the last period

Synthetic Cubism and Classicism and Surrealism

After World War I, Picasso began to work on neoclassicism. One of Picasso's most famous works, Guernica was composed during this period. The painting depicts the Nazi bombing of Guernica during the Spanish Civil War. This is Picasso's development of cubism with the use of collage with fabrics, newspapers, and wallpaper to depict still life subjects and characters.

Analytical Cubism

Analytical Cubism is a painting style that Picasso developed with Georges Braque that uses brownish monochromatic colors for his works. Objects will be separated by two artists into separate parts and "analyzed" according to the shape of this part.
