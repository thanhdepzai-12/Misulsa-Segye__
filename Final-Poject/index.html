    Vincent Van Gogh

    ChildHood

    Vincent Willem van Gogh was born in 1853 in Groot-Zundert, a small village near the city of Breda in the province of North Brabant in the South of the Netherlands. He was the son of Anna Cornelia Carbentus and Theodorus van Gogh, a clergyman of the Dutch Reformed Church. Van Gogh was named after his grandfather and elder brother who had died a year earlier.



    Vincent Van Gogh's family consists of 4 people. His father's brother is Theo Van Gogh and his mother is Anna Carbentus van Gogh. His family is a middle-class family who works in the trade of paintings, so he was exposed to paintings very early.





    When Van Gogh was four years old, his person, Theodorus (Theo) was born on May 1, 1857. He had another son, Cor, and three daughters, Elizabeth, Anna and Wil. When he was a kid, Van Gogh was a quiet, quiet and thoughtful young man. In 1860, he studied in the village of Zundert until 1861, when he began to study at home with his sister Anna under the tutelage of only a female tutor.
